# What is Notion Integration? (Interface to Your Second Brain)

> Notion Integration is a **bidirectional knowledge management pipeline** used to **deposit and extract notes via conversation**, helping **knowledge workers and students** to **record inspiration and retrieve information anytime, anywhere** in **scenarios without switching software**.

---

## In a Nutshell

- **The Problem It Solves**: Too lazy to open Notion to find a page when wanting to write something down, or can't remember where a certain note was saved.
- **Its Core Value**: Frictionless recording (Quick Capture), natural language database retrieval, automatic formatting.
- **Typical Use Cases**: Voice diary while walking, asking AI to read your notes and answer questions, automatically saving chat logs as to-do items.

---

## 1. What is Notion Integration?

Notion integration is essentially **making OpenClaw your Notion secretary**.

You can understand it as **a librarian and stenographer**:
- You just throw ideas, links, tasks to it.
- It automatically categorizes, tags, and fills them into the corresponding Database Property for you.

---

## 2. What Can It Do?

Typically, Notion integration can be used for:

1.  **Light-speed Collection** (One sentence): Say "Save this link to the reading list, tag allows AI", it automatically fills in URL, title, and Tag.
2.  **Knowledge Dialogue**: Ask "What did I write in my 'Marketing Plan' note last month?", it searches and summarizes it for you.
3.  **Content Generation**: Let it write a draft and save it directly to your workspace in Notion Page format (including H1, H2, list).

In actual products, common usages include:
-   **Auto Bookkeeping**: Send "Lunch 30 dollars", automatically fill into your "2026 Ledger" database, amount 30, category Food.
-   **Daily Review**: Ask it to read your TODO list database every night, summarize what was completed today and what is left.
-   **Meeting Minutes**: Send the text converted from meeting recording to it, let it organize into Notion format minutes document.

---

## 3. How to Use?

Generally, using Notion integration involves 3 steps:

1.  **Step 1: Create Connection**
    Create a new `Internal Integration` in Notion's settings page `My connections` and get the Token.

2.  **Step 2: Page Authorization (Most Critical!)**
    Notion's permission design is very strict. You need to go to **the pages or databases you want AI to read/write**, click `...` in the upper right corner -> `Connect to` -> select your integration.
    > **Note**: If you don't do this step, the Bot can't see anything!

3.  **Step 3: Configure OpenClaw**
    Enter the Token in the OpenClaw backend and set the default Database ID to write to (optional).

If you are using it for the first time:
-   **Recommend starting with [Inbox Collection]**: Create a simple "Inbox" database, throw everything you jot down into it first.
-   **Don't [Connect All Pages] at first**: Only authorize necessary pages to ensure data privacy minimization.

---

## 4. Why Use Notion Integration Like This?

The main reasons for recommending operating Notion via conversation are:

-   **Reason 1: Lower Friction**: Notion App starts slow and has complex structure. Sometimes you just want to write down one sentence, the action of opening the App turns you off. Bot is right in the chat window, send instantly.
-   **Reason 2: Structured Ability**: AI can understand natural language ("Remind me next Friday at 3 PM"), and convert it into structured data (Date property), which traditional input methods can't do.
-   **Reason 3: Revitalize Knowledge**: Notes are dead data if not read. AI retrieval allows your forgotten notes to be utilized again.

If you choose manual operation of Notion:
-   Every record requires: Unlock phone -> Open App -> Wait for load -> Find page -> Click New -> Enter title -> Select property... Inspiration runs away long ago.

---

## 5. Who is it For? Who is it Not For?

**Suitable for:**
-   **Notion Power Users**: Practitioners of All-in-one.
-   **GTD Practitioners**: People who need a super-fast Inbox.
-   **Readers/Researchers**: People who need quick archiving of clipped links.

**Not suitable for:**
-   **Layout OCD Patients**: AI-generated page layout may not be perfect and needs manual fine-tuning.
-   **People who hardly use Database**: If your Notion is just plain documents, half the power of integration is lost (Database is the soul of Notion).
